# Manifesto

> A statement of intent for this project and every decision made within it.

---

## We believe that software should help humans think better, not merely faster.

The financial world produces an overwhelming volume of signals, data, and noise every day. Most tools built for this world are designed to accelerate reaction — to make humans respond faster to more information.

This project takes the opposite path.

---

## The Principles

### 1. Understand Before Predicting

Prediction without understanding is noise. Before the system attempts to characterize the future, it must deeply characterize the present. Understanding the *current state* is the first and most important work.

### 2. Diagnose Before Acting

Action without diagnosis is reaction. Every observation of the financial system must pass through a diagnostic lens before any conclusion is drawn. What is the condition? How did it develop? What are the causal forces? Only then can a considered response be formed.

### 3. Observe Before Reacting

The immediate appearance of a signal is rarely the full truth. Markets move. Yields spike. Numbers change. Before reacting, the owner must observe — must sit with the data, contextualize it within historical patterns, and understand its systemic meaning.

### 4. Do Not Chase the Leaves. Understand the Roots.

Asset prices are leaves on a tree. They are visible, they move constantly, and they are easy to focus on. But the leaves are moved by wind that begins in the roots — in monetary policy, in credit systems, in funding markets, in confidence. This system exists to understand the roots, not to track the leaves.

### 5. Every Diagnosis Is Temporary

No diagnosis of the financial system is permanent. Conditions evolve. What is fragile today may stabilize tomorrow. What appears stable today may be accumulating hidden stress. Every diagnosis must carry a timestamp and be open to revision. Certainty is not the goal. Clear thinking is.

### 6. Every Understanding Can Evolve

The owner's mental model is not fixed. As new information arrives, as case studies accumulate, as reflections deepen, understanding must be allowed to grow and change. The system must never lock the owner into yesterday's conclusions.

### 7. Software Should Help Humans Think Better, Not Merely Faster

Speed has its place. But this system is not optimizing for speed. It is optimizing for clarity, depth, and the quality of understanding. If a feature makes the owner react faster but understand less, it does not belong here.

---

## What This Manifesto Protects

This manifesto is the first line of defense against scope creep, feature pressure, and the gravitational pull of convention.

When a new feature is proposed, this manifesto is the first thing to consult.

When a design decision must be made, these principles are the final authority.

When the system risks becoming a trading dashboard — stop. Return here. Read again.

---

## The Commitment

This project commits to building a system that:

- Cultivates understanding over time.
- Rewards deep thinking over fast reacting.
- Grows in wisdom with its owner.
- Remains quiet when silence is the honest answer.
- Stays simple when complexity is the easy path.
