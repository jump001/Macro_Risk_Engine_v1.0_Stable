# Living Knowledge System

> The system is not a database of facts. It is a living record of evolving understanding.

---

## What Is a Living Knowledge System?

A Living Knowledge System is a personal intellectual infrastructure that grows and deepens over time alongside its owner.

It is not static. It does not store facts in frozen form. It captures:

- **Observations** — What has been noticed.
- **Reflections** — What has been thought about.
- **Hypotheses** — What is being tested or suspected.
- **Case Studies** — What history has shown.
- **Journals** — How thinking has evolved over time.

The system is alive because the owner's understanding is alive.

---

## How Knowledge Evolves

Knowledge does not arrive complete. It develops in stages:

```
Raw Data
  → Observation (noticing a pattern)
  → Reflection (thinking about its meaning)
  → Hypothesis (forming a testable belief)
  → Case Study (testing against historical reality)
  → Understanding (internalized knowledge)
  → Revised Understanding (when new evidence arrives)
```

This cycle has no end. Every understanding can evolve. Every conclusion can be revised. The system must support this cycle, not interrupt it.

## The Personal Research Lab

This is not a SaaS dashboard. It is not designed for thousands of users. It is designed for one person.

That distinction matters profoundly:

- The interface serves **one mind's** mental model, not a generic user persona.
- The knowledge grows **alongside one person's** deepening expertise.
- The system's vocabulary — its indicators, states, metaphors — reflects **one person's** framework for understanding the world.

This is a personal research lab. It exists in the tradition of private intellectual journals, research notebooks, and personal knowledge systems that serious thinkers have always kept.

---

## Knowledge Artifacts

The Living Knowledge System produces and preserves several types of artifacts:

### Journals
Dated entries recording what the owner has observed, thought, and felt about the state of the global financial system. Not necessarily analytical — sometimes personal, sometimes exploratory.

### Reflections
Deeper, longer-form thinking pieces. When a pattern has been observed long enough to warrant careful analysis, a reflection is written. Reflections are the output of deep thinking.

### Observations
Short, timestamped records of notable conditions, anomalies, or interesting configurations in the financial system. Raw material for later reflection.

### Hypotheses
Formally stated beliefs about how the financial system works, backed by observations. Hypotheses are held loosely and revised regularly.

### Case Studies
Historical analyses examining past episodes of systemic stress, crisis, or recovery through the lens of this project's framework. Case studies are how the owner learns from history.

## The Knowledge Folder

All knowledge artifacts live in the `/knowledge/` directory:

```
knowledge/
├── journals/          — Dated entries and personal observations
├── reflections/       — Long-form analytical writing
├── observations/      — Short-form timestamped notices
├── hypotheses/        — Formally stated beliefs under test
└── case-studies/      — Historical analyses
```

These are plain text/markdown files. They are version controlled. They grow over time. They are irreplaceable.

## The Relationship Between Knowledge and the Risk Engine

The Risk Engine provides structured, quantitative signals.

The Living Knowledge System provides context, narrative, and evolving interpretation.

Neither replaces the other. Together they form a complete picture:

- The Risk Engine says: *SOFR stress is elevated, Treasury auction is weak — the system is in FRAGILE state.*
- The Living Knowledge System says: *This is the third time in eighteen months this pattern has appeared. The previous two times it resolved within six weeks. The difference this time is the concurrent DXY strength, which was not present before.*

One without the other is incomplete. The Risk Engine without the Living Knowledge System is just numbers. The Living Knowledge System without the Risk Engine is just opinion.

The system is not finished when it is deployed. It is never finished.

As the owner's understanding deepens:
- New hypotheses will be added.
- Old case studies will be revisited and revised.
