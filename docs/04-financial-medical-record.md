# Financial Medical Record

> The global financial system, understood as a living patient with measurable vital signs, diagnosable conditions, and treatable crises.

---

## The Medical Metaphor

The medical metaphor is not decorative. It is a framework for thinking.

Medicine has developed, over centuries, a systematic methodology for understanding complex living systems:

- Observe vital signs.
- Identify symptoms.
- Form a diagnosis.
- Propose treatment.
- Monitor response.
- Update prognosis.

This methodology is exactly what macro-financial analysis requires. The global financial system is a complex living system. It has vital signs. It experiences stress. It can become fragile. It can require intervention. It can recover.

The Financial Medical Record is the application of this medical framework to the global financial system.

---

## The Patient

**Patient:** The Global Financial System

The patient is vast and interconnected. Its health cannot be measured by any single indicator. A complete picture requires monitoring multiple vital signs simultaneously, understanding how they interact, and recognizing patterns that indicate systemic stress.

## Medical States

### Healthy
All vital signs within normal ranges. The system is functioning as expected. No intervention required.

### Watch
One or more indicators showing mild elevation. Not alarming, but warranting closer attention. Like a patient with slightly elevated blood pressure — monitor, do not panic.

### Fragile
Multiple indicators showing elevated stress. The system is under strain. The probability of deterioration is increasing. Begin diagnostic review.

### Pre-Crisis
Indicators showing significant stress across multiple vital signs. The pattern is consistent with conditions that historically precede crisis. Heightened vigilance required.

### Critical
Acute systemic stress. Multiple vital signs critically elevated. The system is in an acute event or very close to one.

### Deleveraging
The forced unwinding of leveraged positions across the system. A cascade event. The financial equivalent of a severe systemic infection — the immune system is responding, but the patient is under severe stress.

## Interventions and Treatments

| Medical Treatment | Financial Equivalent | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Liquidity Injection | Quantitative Easing (QE) | Central bank purchases assets to inject money into the system |
| Emergency Blood Transfer | Fed/Central Bank Swap Lines | Emergency currency agreements between central banks |
| Emergency Room | Emergency Lending Facilities | Lender of last resort operations |
| Stabilizer | Deposit Guarantee | Government-backed deposit insurance to prevent bank runs |
| Life Support | Yield Curve Control (YCC) | Central bank directly controls interest rates across the yield curve |
| Nutritional Support | Fiscal Stimulus | Government spending to support economic activity |

## Diagnostic Framework

### Intake Assessment
When the owner opens the system, the first view is an intake assessment — a summary of the patient's current vital signs and overall health state.

### Symptom Review
Which indicators are elevated? By how much? For how long?

### Differential Diagnosis
What conditions could explain this pattern of symptoms? Historical case studies are consulted.

### Working Diagnosis
The current best explanation of the system's condition, held provisionally.

### Treatment History
What interventions have central banks and governments deployed? Are they working?

### Prognosis
Based on current conditions and historical patterns, how is this likely to evolve?

## The Financial Immune System

The financial system has its own immune response — mechanisms that activate under stress to restore stability:

- **Central bank intervention** — the first responder.
- **Government fiscal response** — the longer-term healer.
- **Market self-correction** — deleveraging that clears excess.
- **International coordination** — collective response to global stress.

Understanding when the immune system is active, whether it is effective, and whether it may itself cause side effects is part of the complete diagnosis.

---

## Why This Metaphor Matters

The medical metaphor does something important: it removes the speculative, gambling-oriented framing from financial analysis.

A doctor examining a patient is not trying to profit from the patient's condition. The doctor is trying to understand it clearly and, if possible, help.

This system adopts the same posture. The owner is the doctor. The global financial system is the patient. The goal is understanding and, where possible, diagnosis — not speculation about which asset to buy.
