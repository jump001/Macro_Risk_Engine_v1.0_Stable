# Hypotheses

> Formally stated beliefs about how the financial system works, backed by observations, held provisionally, and revised with evidence.

---

## Purpose

A hypothesis is a testable belief. It is more structured than a journal entry and more explicit than a reflection.

A hypothesis says: *I believe this is true. Here is the evidence. Here is what would change my mind.*

Hypotheses are held loosely. The expectation is that they will be revised. A revised hypothesis is not a failure — it is the system working correctly.

---

## Format

```markdown
# Hypothesis: [Short Title]

**Created:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Status:** Active | Confirmed | Revised | Rejected
**Last Reviewed:** YYYY-MM-DD

## Statement
[One clear sentence: "I believe that X happens when Y because Z."]

## Evidence Supporting This
[List of observations, case studies, or data patterns that support this]

## Evidence Against This
[Honest listing of evidence that contradicts or complicates this]

## What Would Confirm This
[Specific conditions that, if observed, would strengthen this hypothesis]

## What Would Revise or Reject This
[Specific conditions that, if observed, would require changing or abandoning this]

## Current Status Notes
[Most recent thinking on this hypothesis]

## Revision History
[Brief dated notes when hypothesis is updated]
```

---

## Naming Convention

Files should be named descriptively: `H-NNN-short-description.md`

Example: `H-001-yen-carry-unwind-leads-global-selloff.md`

---

## Principle

> A hypothesis held too firmly becomes a bias.
> A hypothesis never formed is just a feeling.
> Write it down. Test it. Revise it. That is how understanding deepens.
