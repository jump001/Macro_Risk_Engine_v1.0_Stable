# Observations

> Short, timestamped notices of notable conditions, anomalies, or interesting configurations in the financial system.

---

## Purpose

An observation is a brief, factual record of something noticed. It is not analysis — that belongs in Reflections. It is not opinion — that belongs in Journals.

An observation is just: *at this time, this was happening.*

Observations are the raw material from which reflections and hypotheses are eventually built. A single observation may seem unremarkable. A pattern of observations across time becomes significant.

---

## Format

```markdown
# Observation: [Short Title]

**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Indicators:** [List of affected indicators]
**Risk State:** [Current Risk State]

## What Was Noticed
[1-3 sentences describing the specific condition or anomaly]

## Why It Seems Notable
[Brief — 1-2 sentences. If not sure, that's fine to say.]

## Tags
[Keywords: e.g., sofr, dxy, treasury-auction, funding-stress, yen-carry]
```

---

## Naming Convention

Files should be named: `YYYY-MM-DD-short-description.md`

Example: `2026-06-27-sofr-elevated-treasury-auction-weak.md`

---

## Principle

> Observe without interpreting. Record what is happening before deciding what it means.
> The interpretation comes later, in a Reflection.
